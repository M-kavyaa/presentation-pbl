MediStock: Centralized Jan Aushadhi Inventory

"Developing a web-based system to provide affordable healthcare through real-time monitoring, reducing medicine wastage, and improving stock balances across PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras."

Introduction

PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras aim to provide affordable essential medicines to the public, improving access to healthcare and reducing out-of-pocket expenses. However, these Kendras face challenges like medicine expiry, stock imbalances, and decentralized inventory management, leading to significant wastage and shortages.

The Problem

  • Manual Waste: High wastage due to poor batch tracking and manual monitoring.
  • Decentralized Imbalance: Overstocking in some locations while others face critical shortages.
  • Forced Alternatives: Shortages force patients to buy costly commercial alternatives.
  • Audit Failures: Audits found over 50% of medicines expired at specific Kendras due to poor monitoring.

The Innovation

A centralized web-based system matching "High-Expiry Risk" batches with "High-Demand" status in nearby nodes.

Goal: Transform fragmented inventory into a unified, responsive supply chain network.

Literature Review

Wastage & Tracking Studies

Patel et al. (2022): Studies confirm high medicine wastage is a direct result of manual batch tracking failures.

Kumar & Singh (2021): Decentralized inventory models consistently show overstocking issues.

Times of India (2023): Multiple reports of patients being forced into costly commercial purchases due to Kendra shortages.

Solutions & Best Practices

Raghavan et al. (2020): Centralized monitoring enables effective FEFO practices and stock redistribution.

Sharma & Gupta (2021): Data-driven forecasting using historical sales drastically reduces expiry-related losses.

WHO Report (2020): Optimized distribution via early-warning alerts is critical for public health supply chains.

Project Objectives

Minimize medicine wastage through automated tracking.

Centrally monitor stock levels across all Kendras.

Generate real-time expiry alerts and FEFO suggestions.

Enable smart stock transfer recommendations between shops.

Track sales trends and predict future medicine demand.

Improve overall availability of low-cost medicines.
